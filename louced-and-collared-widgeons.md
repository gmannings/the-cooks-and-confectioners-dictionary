# Louced and Collared Widgeons

## Description
Louced and Collared Widgeons is a dish made from boneless widgeon meat that is marinated, seasoned, rolled into collars, and then boiled in a flavorful broth. The dish is garnished with minced Weftphalia-ham.

## Ingredients:
- 2 widgeons, boneless
- 200 ml white wine
- 4 cloves
- 1/2 teaspoon ground mace
- 1/2 teaspoon ground pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon minced sage
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 2 onions, sliced
- 500 ml strong broth (chicken or vegetable)
- 1/2 teaspoon whole peppercorns
- 2-3 large mace blades
- 100 g Weftphalia-ham, minced

## Instructions:
1. In a bowl, combine the white wine, cloves, ground mace, ground pepper, and salt.
2. Place the boneless widgeon meat in the marinade and let it soak for 12 hours in the refrigerator.
3. Remove the widgeon meat from the marinade and pat it dry.
4. Season the widgeon meat with ground pepper and minced sage.
5. Dip the seasoned widgeon meat in the beaten eggs, ensuring it is coated evenly.
6. Lay the widgeon meat on its sides and place a couple of onion slices on top.
7. Roll up the widgeon meat into collars, securing them with kitchen twine if necessary.
8. In a large pot, combine the strong broth, white wine, whole peppercorns, and large mace blades to create the pickle.
9. Bring the pickle to a boil and carefully add the widgeon collars.
10. Reduce the heat to a simmer and cook the widgeon collars for about 1 hour, or until they are cooked through.
11. Remove the widgeon collars from the pickle and let them cool slightly.
12. Remove the kitchen twine from the collars and slice them into rounds.
13. Serve the louced and collared widgeons garnished with minced Weftphalia-ham.

## Cooking time
Approximately 1 hour
