# Ragoo of Sturgeon

## Description
A ragoo is a rich and flavorful stew-like dish. This recipe calls for sturgeon, a type of fish, which is cut into pieces and cooked with a variety of ingredients to create a delicious and hearty dish.

## Ingredients:
- 500 grams sturgeon fillets
- 100 grams bacon, diced
- 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 250 ml beef or fish broth
- 100 grams mushrooms, sliced
- 50 grams truffles, sliced (optional)
- 2 veal sweetbreads, blanched and sliced
- 1 tablespoon fresh herbs (such as parsley, thyme, and bay leaves), chopped
- 1 lemon, sliced
- Salt and pepper to taste
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch (for thickening)
- 2 tablespoons verjuice (or lemon juice)

## Instructions:
1. Cut the sturgeon fillets into bite-sized pieces.
2. Lard the sturgeon pieces by inserting small strips of bacon into them.
3. Drudge the larded sturgeon pieces with flour, coating them evenly.
4. Heat the vegetable oil in a large frying pan over medium heat.
5. Fry the sturgeon pieces until they are browned on all sides. Remove them from the pan and set aside.
6. In a stew-pan, combine the beef or fish broth, mushrooms, truffles, veal sweetbreads, fresh herbs, and slices of lemon.
7. Add the fried sturgeon pieces to the stew-pan.
8. Cover the stew-pan and let the ingredients stew over low heat for about 30 minutes, or until the sturgeon is cooked through and tender.
9. In a small bowl, mix the cornstarch with a little water to create a slurry.
10. Remove the stew-pan from the heat and gradually stir in the cornstarch slurry to thicken the sauce.
11. Return the stew-pan to low heat and cook for an additional 5 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
12. Skim off any excess fat from the surface of the sauce.
13. Stir in the verjuice (or lemon juice) to add a tangy flavor to the ragoo.
14. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
15. Serve the ragoo of sturgeon hot, garnished with fresh herbs if desired.

## Cooking time
Approximately 1 hour
