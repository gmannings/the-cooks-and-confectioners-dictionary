# Fried Turbot

## Description
Fried Turbot is a classic seafood dish where slices of turbot are coated in flour and fried until golden brown. The fish is then served with a flavorful sauce made with wine, anchovy, spices, and lemon.

## Ingredients:
- 1 turbot fillet, sliced
- Flour, for coating
- Clarified butter, for frying
- 1 cup claret or white wine
- 1 anchovy fillet, minced
- Salt, to taste
- Nutmeg, to taste
- Ginger, to taste
- A pinch of saffron
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1 lemon, minced
- 1 shallot or onion or clove of garlic, for rubbing the dish

## Instructions:
1. Slice the turbot fillet into ribbed pieces.
2. Coat the turbot slices in flour, shaking off any excess.
3. Heat clarified butter in a frying pan over medium heat.
4. Fry the turbot slices in the butter until they begin to turn brown. Flip them halfway through to ensure even cooking.
5. Remove the fried turbot from the pan and drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
6. Clean the frying pan and return it to the heat.
7. Pour in the claret or white wine, minced anchovy, salt, nutmeg, ginger, and saffron.
8. Add the fried turbot slices back into the pan and cook until half of the liquid has evaporated.
9. Add the butter and minced lemon to the pan and mix everything together.
10. Rub a dish with a shallot, onion, or clove of garlic to impart flavor.
11. Transfer the fried turbot slices and the sauce to the prepared dish.
12. Serve the fried turbot hot.

## Cooking time
Approximately 30 minutes.
